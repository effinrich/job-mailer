# Voice & structure

How these emails should sound, and the skeletons to start from. These are *guides*,
not mail-merge templates — every email gets real per-person research in the hook.

## Principles

1. **Their world first, your relevance second, a small ask third.** Never open with
   "I'm a software engineer looking for…". Open with something true about *them*.
2. **One specific hook beats any amount of polish.** If the first line could be sent
   to 100 people, it's not done.
3. **Short.** 5–8 sentences. They're busy. Respect it.
4. **A low-friction ask.** "15 minutes," "whether or not it leads to the role," "no
   worries if not" — make yes easy and no painless.
5. **Sound like a person, not a pitch.** Contractions, plain words, no "I hope this
   email finds you well," no buzzwords.
6. **Never fake familiarity or invent facts.** If the research isn't there, don't send.

## Skeleton A — direct (there's an open role on their team)

> Subject: {specific detail from their work}
>
> Hi {name} — {one true, specific observation about them or their team}. {One
> sentence connecting that to a thing you've actually done.} I saw {the role/team}
> and would love to {small ask — 15 min, learn more}, whether or not it's a fit.
>
> {signature}

## Skeleton B — informational (no posted role, you just want in)

> Subject: {their work} + a quick question
>
> Hi {name} — {true specific observation}. I'm exploring {your direction} and the way
> {their team/company} approaches {thing} is exactly the kind of work I want to be
> near. Could I ask you {one sharp question} / grab 15 minutes? Totally fine if you're
> swamped.
>
> {signature}

## Skeleton C — warm follow-up (no reply yet)

> Subject: re: {original subject}
>
> Hi {name} — floating this back up in case it slipped by. {Optional: one new, tiny
> relevant nugget.} Still happy to keep it to 15 minutes. If now's not the time, no
> worries at all — I'll leave it here.
>
> {signature}
